# **Data Professional Survey — Workforce Insights Dashboard**

### **Project Overview**

This dashboard analyses a global survey of **630 data professionals** collected in June 2022, covering roles, salaries, job satisfaction, career transitions, and programming preferences. The goal is to surface trends that help organisations understand the realities of the data workforce — what drives people in, what keeps them, and what pushes them out.

---

### **Data Source**

- **Source:** Data Professional Survey (self-reported, MR.Alex Freberg)
- **Period:** June 2022
- **Total Respondents:** 630
- **Geographic Spread:** 40+ countries, primarily United States, India, United Kingdom, and Canada

---

### **Tools Used**

- **Power BI Desktop** — data modelling, DAX measures, interactive dashboard
- **Microsoft Excel** — raw data storage and initial review

---

### **Key Findings**

- **59% of respondents switched careers** into data from another field
- **Python dominates** as the favourite language, chosen by 67% of respondents
- **Salary satisfaction is the lowest-rated metric** across all happiness dimensions (avg 4.27/10)
- **Work/life balance and coworkers** are the highest-rated satisfaction areas (5.74 and 5.86/10)
- **47% cite "Better Salary"** as the most important factor if searching for a new job today
- **Breaking in is seen as hard** — over 31% rated the experience as Difficult or Very Difficult
- The **average data professional is 29 years old**, with most holding a Bachelor's or Master's degree

| Job Satisfaction | Happiness scores across 6 dimensions |
| Career Entry | Difficulty breaking in, career switchers vs. direct entrants |
